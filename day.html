A Day in the Life

Following an NHS Information Analyst through a real working day.

8:30 AM – Starting the day

The day begins by checking emails and reviewing urgent requests from NHS departments. Analysts often receive data queries from hospital managers needing fast answers for decision-making.

9:00 AM – Data extraction

The analyst accesses NHS databases and extracts patient or performance data. This may include waiting times, bed occupancy, or treatment success rates.

10:30 AM – Cleaning and preparing data

Raw NHS data is often messy. Analysts clean datasets by removing errors, duplicates, and missing values. This step is essential before any analysis can be done.

12:00 PM – Building dashboards

Using tools like Power BI or Excel, the analyst creates visual dashboards. These dashboards show trends such as patient waiting times or hospital performance.

1:00 PM – Lunch break

A short break to reset before the afternoon workload. NHS environments are often fast-paced, so breaks help maintain focus and accuracy.

2:00 PM – Meeting with NHS staff

Analysts meet with managers, doctors, or department heads to explain findings. Communication is key — data must be translated into simple, actionable insights.

3:30 PM – Problem solving & analysis

The analyst investigates trends or issues raised earlier in the day. For example, why waiting times increased or why a department is underperforming.

4:30 PM – Reporting & documentation

Final reports are written and sent to NHS teams. These reports directly influence hospital planning and patient care decisions.

Why this role matters daily

Every single day, NHS Information Analysts help improve healthcare systems. Their work ensures decisions are based on evidence, not guesswork — which directly impacts patient lives.
