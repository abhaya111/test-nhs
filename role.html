The Role of an NHS Information Analyst

Turning healthcare data into decisions that improve patient care and NHS efficiency.

What they do

NHS Information Analysts work with large sets of healthcare data and turn it into meaningful insights. They help hospitals understand performance, patient flow, and demand.

Their role ensures data is not just stored, but actively used to improve healthcare systems.

How they do it

They use tools such as Excel, SQL, and Power BI to analyse NHS data. This includes cleaning raw datasets, identifying patterns, and building dashboards.

These visuals make complex healthcare data easy for staff to understand and act on.

What they produce

  • Performance reports for hospitals
  • Dashboards showing waiting times
  • Trend analysis for patient flow
  • Forecasts for healthcare demand

Why it is important

The NHS produces huge volumes of data every day. Without analysts, this data would remain unused.

Information Analysts help reduce waiting times, improve efficiency, and support better clinical decisions.

Impact on healthcare

Their work enables the NHS to move from guesswork to data-driven decision making.

This improves planning, resource allocation, and patient outcomes across the system.
